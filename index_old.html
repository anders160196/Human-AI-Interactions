Intermittent

The intermittent interaction is initiated by the user providing an explicit request to the system. It is then terminated shortly after when the system has delivered on the request.

Example: AI system that suggest music to play when requested by the user.


Continuous

The continuous interaction typically have a longer time duration. The interaction is a process rather than a single task, in which the user and the system are actively collaborating, exchanging information and coordinating actions.

Example: AI system that continually support users who are actively exploring new music.


Proactive

The proactive interaction is initiated by the system, not by the user. The interaction will be triggered by a sensor or change in internal system state, resulting in a system response. The user may or may not be aware or react to the interference of AI.

Example: AI system that automatically play appropriate music to match the mood of the users.
